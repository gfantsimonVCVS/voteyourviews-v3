# Candidate Positions Guide
## VoteYourViews.org — Texas 2026

---

## The Position System

Each candidate is coded **A**, **B**, **C**, or **D** for each of the 9 issues.

| Code | Label | Color | Meaning |
|------|-------|-------|---------|
| **A** | Liberal | 🔵 Blue | Supports the progressive/liberal position on this issue |
| **B** | Moderate | 🟣 Purple | Holds a middle-ground or nuanced position |
| **C** | Conservative | 🔴 Red | Supports the conservative/traditional position on this issue |
| **D** | N/A | ⚫ Gray | Has not taken a position, or this issue does not apply |

## The 9 Issues

### 1. `reproductiveRights` — Reproductive Rights
- **A (Liberal):** Supports abortion access; opposes Texas's total ban; believes decisions belong to patients and doctors
- **B (Moderate):** Supports abortion in early pregnancy with exceptions; opposes total ban but accepts some limits
- **C (Conservative):** Supports Texas's abortion ban; believes life begins at conception; government should protect the unborn
- **D:** Has not publicly stated a position

### 2. `immigration` — Immigration & Border
- **A (Liberal):** Believes immigrants strengthen Texas; supports humane legal pathways; opposes mass deportation
- **B (Moderate):** Supports secure borders AND a realistic path for people already here; firm but humane enforcement
- **C (Conservative):** Border security is top priority; strong enforcement; opposes illegal immigration
- **D:** Has not publicly stated a position

### 3. `gunPolicy` — Gun Policy
- **A (Liberal):** Supports universal background checks, red flag laws, assault weapon restrictions
- **B (Moderate):** Supports Second Amendment AND believes basic safety measures (background checks) are reasonable
- **C (Conservative):** Second Amendment must be fully protected; opposes gun restrictions
- **D:** Has not publicly stated a position

### 4. `education` — Public Education
- **A (Liberal):** Supports fully funding public schools; opposes vouchers/school choice diverting public funds
- **B (Moderate):** Supports public schools but open to some parental choice options with accountability
- **C (Conservative):** Supports school vouchers and parental choice; questions public school system's performance
- **D:** Has not publicly stated a position

### 5. `healthcare` — Healthcare
- **A (Liberal):** Supports Medicaid expansion; believes healthcare is a right; favors government healthcare programs
- **B (Moderate):** Supports some expansion of healthcare access; wants market solutions alongside public programs
- **C (Conservative):** Opposes Medicaid expansion; favors free market healthcare; opposes government mandates
- **D:** Has not publicly stated a position

### 6. `climate` — Climate & Energy
- **A (Liberal):** Acknowledges human-caused climate change; supports clean energy transition; opposes fossil fuel expansion
- **B (Moderate):** Believes in some climate action while maintaining Texas energy economy; "all of the above" approach
- **C (Conservative):** Prioritizes Texas oil/gas economy; skeptical of climate regulations; energy independence first
- **D:** Has not publicly stated a position

### 7. `moneyInPolitics` — Money in Politics
- **A (Liberal):** Supports campaign finance reform; wants donor transparency; opposes PAC/special interest influence
- **B (Moderate):** Supports some transparency measures; mixed views on campaign finance limits
- **C (Conservative):** Opposes campaign finance restrictions as free speech; skeptical of reform efforts
- **D:** Has not publicly stated a position

### 8. `socialSecurity` — Social Security & Medicare
- **A (Liberal):** Fully protect Social Security and Medicare; expand benefits; no cuts
- **B (Moderate):** Protect core benefits but open to some structural reform to ensure solvency
- **C (Conservative):** Open to restructuring or privatizing; concerned about long-term cost; favors market-based solutions
- **D:** Has not publicly stated a position

### 9. `affordability` — Housing & Affordability
- **A (Liberal):** Supports government intervention to lower housing costs; tenant protections; affordable housing programs
- **B (Moderate):** Supports some market reforms and targeted assistance; mixed approach
- **C (Conservative):** Favors deregulation and market solutions; opposes rent control; property rights first
- **D:** Has not publicly stated a position

## How to Determine a Candidate's Position

### Priority Order (use the strongest available evidence):

1. **Direct quote on the specific issue** — candidate's own words in an interview, speech, press release, or debate
2. **Voting record** — how they voted on relevant legislation (incumbents only)
3. **Campaign platform** — published position on their official website
4. **Endorsements** — what organizations have endorsed them (e.g., NRA endorsement = C on Gun Policy)
5. **Party affiliation** — use as a last resort; not all candidates follow party lines

### When to use B (Moderate):
Only assign **B** if there is genuine evidence the candidate holds a nuanced position that doesn't fit A or C. Do NOT use B as a default placeholder. If you're uncertain, use D until you have evidence.

### When to use D (N/A):
- Candidate has made no public statements on this issue
- Issue genuinely doesn't apply to the office (rare)
- You cannot find reliable sourcing to assign A, B, or C

## Quotes & Sources Standard

Every A/B/C position should eventually have at least one source. Goal is direct candidate quotes.

### Acceptable sources (in order of preference):
1. **Direct quote** — candidate's own words, verbatim, with quotation marks
2. **Voting record** — specific bill name, vote date, legislative session
3. **Campaign website** — candidate's published platform (note: may change)
4. **News reporting** — reputable outlet covering the candidate's stated position
5. **Endorsement/Questionnaire** — e.g., "Received A rating from NRA" or "Completed Planned Parenthood questionnaire"

### What NOT to use:
- Social media posts without verification
- Anonymous sources
- Paraphrased summaries without a primary source link
- Partisan attack ads or opposition research

### Source notation format (for the Sources sheet):
| Field | Example |
|-------|---------|
| `name` | James Talarico |
| `issueId` | reproductiveRights |
| `quote` | "Texas's current total ban goes too far and puts lives at risk." |
| `source` | Texas Tribune, Interview |
| `url` | https://texastribune.org/... |
| `date` | 2025-03-12 |

Multiple sources for the same candidate + issue = multiple rows.

## Editorial Standard

> **The app does not speak for candidates.**

When displaying a candidate's position:
- **If a real quote exists:** show it in quotation marks with attribution — *"quote text." — Candidate Name, Source*
- **If no quote exists:** show "Source coming soon" — do NOT paraphrase or summarize as if it were the candidate's words
- **Position labels (A/B/C)** are the app's categorization, not the candidate's self-description

This standard protects the app's credibility and the candidate's right to speak for themselves.

---

## Who Can Edit Positions

Position assignments (A/B/C) should be reviewed by at least one editor before publishing. Any change from B to A or C, or from A/C to B (moderate), requires a source citation to justify the nuance.
